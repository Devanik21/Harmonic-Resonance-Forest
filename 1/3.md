## The Invention: HarmonicResonanceClassifier
At its core, HRF treats data points as "particles" in a resonance field:
- **Wave Potential:** For a query point \( x \), compute distances to training points per class, then modulate with frequency \( f = \text{base_freq} \times (\text{class_id} + 1) \) and damping (e.g., Gaussian decay).
- **Resonance Energy:** Sum \( \frac{1}{1 + d} \cos(f \cdot d) \) over points, where \( d \) is distance. Predict the class with maximum energy.
- **Physics Twist:** Inspired by quantum superpositionâ€”waves interfere constructively near class clusters, destructively elsewhere.

We benchmark against scikit-learn classics on `make_moons(n_samples=300, noise=0.2)`, a non-linear XOR-like dataset where linear models falter.

**Dataset Snapshot:** Two interlocking half-circles, 70/30 train/test split.
